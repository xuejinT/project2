What is Panda Diplomacy?

It’s called “panda diplomacy” and it’s thought to have started as early as the Tang Dynasty in the 7th century when Empress Wu Zeitan sent a pair of bears (believed to be pandas) to Japan. It is China's use of giant pandas as political gifts overseas. These black and white diplomats have gone a long way to undo years of conflict.


Timeline

Why did the policy change after 1990?

Since 1990, pandas have been sent out to other countries as a loan for cooperative scientific research. In other words, China started charging the receiving country for pandas instead of giving out pandas as gifts.

Around the year 1988, some protests emerged saying that giant pandas going overseas had caused both the individual (since some pandas going abroad were for short exhibition) and the whole species some problems. As supported statistically, the number of giant pandas started to decline around 1985. On top of that, there were neither enough founding nor policies for giant panda conservation, while the panda was being declared as endangered in 1990.

In the same year, several laws were enforced and helped their chance of survival. The probation of short time exhibition and improvement of the reserves' standard were also included.

Finally the cooperative scientific research agreement was reached in 1990, and the money that the Chinese government received are exclusively used in the protection of giant pandas. Thanks to all the efforts that giant pandas were reclassified from "endangered" to "vulnerable" in 2016.

What's in coorperative scientific research agreement?

(Or how much does it cost to loan a giant panda?)

The agreement only allows to loan the pandas in pairs for 10 years (renewal is allowed).

In exchange, the receiving country will pay for 1 million dollars per year to China for 10 years (the loan period) and work closely with Chinese scientists for a cooperative research.

Plus, each offspring of the loaned pandas remains property of China, and there will be an extra cub tax of $600,000 for each baby panda. Furthermore, the baby pandas need to return to China at the age of 3.
